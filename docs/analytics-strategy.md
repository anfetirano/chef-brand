# Analytics Strategy

## Analytics objective

Analytics should help answer whether the platform is attracting the right people, from the right places, with the right level of intent.

The goal is not vanity reporting. The goal is strategic learning.

## Core measurement philosophy

The platform should measure behavior that indicates:

- Discovery
- Relevance
- Trust
- Intent
- Conversion

Metrics should always be interpreted in relation to opportunity quality, not just raw traffic volume.

## Key metrics

### Visitors

This measures overall discovery and reach.

Why it matters:

- Shows whether visibility is growing
- Helps compare traffic changes over time
- Creates baseline understanding of awareness

### Countries

This measures geographic relevance.

Why it matters:

- Confirms whether traffic is aligned with target markets
- Helps evaluate progress toward Canada-focused visibility
- Supports future international expansion decisions

### Cities

This adds more precise geographic context.

Why it matters:

- Helps identify clusters of interest in relevant hospitality regions
- Can reveal strong interest from target cities
- Supports future localized messaging

### Traffic source

This measures where visitors come from.

Why it matters:

- Reveals which channels are creating qualified attention
- Helps compare search, direct, referral, social, and outreach traffic
- Supports smarter investment in visibility efforts

### Time on page

This measures engagement depth.

Why it matters:

- Indicates whether content is holding attention
- Helps identify which pages build trust best
- Can reveal weak messaging or weak structure when engagement is low

### Downloads

This measures interest in deeper evaluation materials such as a resume.

Why it matters:

- Signals a stronger level of professional consideration
- Helps identify serious employer behavior
- Connects content consumption with conversion intent

### WhatsApp clicks

This measures direct-contact intent through an informal but high-conversion channel.

Why it matters:

- Shows immediate willingness to initiate contact
- Can indicate high urgency or strong interest
- Helps compare how different contact methods perform

### Email clicks

This measures formal professional contact intent.

Why it matters:

- Often indicates serious outreach behavior
- Supports understanding of conversion quality
- Can be tied to page and source context for insight

## Secondary future metrics

Over time, analytics may also measure:

- Scroll depth
- Section engagement
- Repeat visitors
- Device patterns
- Exit pages
- Resume view versus download behavior
- Employer dashboard access behavior

These should only be added when they support real decisions.

## Future private dashboard

A future private dashboard should turn raw metrics into strategic insight.

It may eventually show:

- High-interest regions
- Best-performing trust pages
- Most effective traffic channels
- Conversion path performance
- Download and contact trends
- Opportunity source attribution

This dashboard should help Andres or collaborators understand where real professional interest is forming.

## Data principles

- Track only what informs decisions
- Prefer clarity over metric overload
- Connect measurement to trust and conversion
- Preserve a clean, professional user experience
- Build analytics as a product capability, not an afterthought
