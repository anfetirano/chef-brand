# Brand Guidelines

## Brand objective

The brand should make employers and sponsors feel that Andres is a serious, capable, and memorable professional. It must create confidence before conversation.

## Brand philosophy

The brand should follow a simple principle:

Do not sell what Andres does. Sell who Andres is.

The experience should not merely communicate culinary ability. It should communicate standards, identity, discipline, and readiness.

## Tone

The tone should be:

- Confident
- Precise
- Warm
- Mature
- Intentional

It should never be:

- Loud
- Desperate
- Overly formal
- Overly casual
- Self-congratulatory

## Personality

The brand personality should feel:

- Professional
- Refined
- Human
- Focused
- Reliable
- Distinctive

The goal is to feel premium through restraint and quality.

## Writing style

Writing should be:

- Clear
- Direct
- Thoughtful
- Elegant without being ornamental
- Specific rather than generic

Preferred writing traits:

- Strong verbs
- Short to medium-length sentences
- Concrete professional language
- Emotion through conviction, not exaggeration

## Photography style

Photography should communicate standards and atmosphere.

Future visual direction should prefer:

- Clean composition
- Real professional context
- Texture and craft
- Controlled lighting
- Authentic kitchen and plating moments
- Images that feel editorial rather than promotional

Photography should avoid:

- Visual clutter
- Overprocessed imagery
- Stock-photo energy
- Random lifestyle filler

## Visual identity

The visual identity should feel disciplined and premium.

It should suggest:

- Precision
- Taste
- Craft
- Composure

The brand should avoid looking trendy for its own sake. Its visual choices should age well and reinforce professional seriousness.

## Employer perception

The desired employer perception is:

- This chef is credible
- This chef has standards
- This chef presents himself seriously
- This chef feels ready for opportunity
- This chef is worth meeting

Everything in the brand should work toward this perception.

## Future evolution

As the platform grows, the brand can become richer without losing discipline.

Possible future expansions include:

- More nuanced storytelling
- Market-specific messaging
- Multilingual tone systems
- Sponsor-facing adaptations
- Employer-dashboard language and presentation standards

Growth should deepen the brand, not dilute it.
