# Product Principles

These principles are immutable. If a future decision conflicts with them, the decision should be reconsidered.

## 1. Trust before aesthetics

Beauty matters, but only when it reinforces credibility. Visual decisions must support confidence, not distract from it.

## 2. Story before resume

Facts alone do not create conviction. The platform should frame experience through narrative, context, and meaning.

## 3. Human before corporate

Andres should feel like a real person with discipline, standards, and personality. The brand must never become generic or over-polished to the point of feeling artificial.

## 4. Performance over visual effects

Speed, clarity, responsiveness, and reliability matter more than decorative motion or technical novelty.

## 5. Mobile first

Decision-makers will often discover the profile on mobile. The experience must earn trust immediately on small screens.

## 6. SEO first

If the right people cannot discover the platform, the brand loses opportunity. Search visibility is a strategic requirement, not a marketing extra.

## 7. Every section must increase trust

No section should exist only to fill space. Each one must strengthen credibility, clarity, or emotional confidence.

## 8. Every interaction must have a purpose

Buttons, links, downloads, and contact points should move visitors toward understanding or action.

## 9. Clarity over cleverness

The platform should be memorable because it is well considered, not because it is confusingly creative.

## 10. Premium through discipline

The premium feeling should come from restraint, quality, consistency, and detail rather than excess.

## 11. Identity over self-promotion

The brand should express who Andres is, how he works, and why he matters. It should never feel like loud self-advertising.

## 12. Global readiness from the beginning

Even if the immediate objective is Canada, the product foundation should be compatible with future international positioning, multilingual expansion, and broader opportunity types.
